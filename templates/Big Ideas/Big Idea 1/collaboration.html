Big Idea 1: Collaboration

In computer science, little can be accomplished alone. Forming a good team is a vital aspect of the development process. This means finding people who specialize in a variety of different fields, and have different backgrounds. Having different perspectives during the development process will also help you attract a diverse userbase, which in turn will lead to even better feedback and features in the future.

This is the brainwrite our team worked on when we planned out our website. Each person put their ideas for the categories in their assigned row, then as a team we went through each column to decide which features to include.

This is our team's Scrum Board. It's a visual organizer that helps us keep track of tasks. We have 6 columns to accommodate for live grading, but you really only need the main three: backlog, in-progress, and completed. On Github, you can label issues to sort them, assign people to certain issues, and use Markdown checklists to track progress.
This photo is an example of a standup meeting. Standup meetings are meant to be short planning sessions (the standing alleviates the discomfort of discussing while sitting). They are a great way to make sure that everyone is up to speed and understands what must be done by certain times.
